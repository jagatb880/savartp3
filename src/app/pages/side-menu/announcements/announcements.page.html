Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)
An asset management company (AMC) is a company that invests the funds received from its clients into securities that match actual financial objectives of the company itself. With asset management companies, individual investors are fed with more diversification and investing options than they would have otherwise been able to avail by themselves. AMCs manage pension plans, mutual funds, hedge funds and these companies earn income by charging set fees/service fees or commissions or say charge a percentage of the total assets under management (AUM). AMCs pool up the assets together and pay out proportional returns and allow the investors to avoid minimum investment requirements, which may be quite high when purchased individually. AMCs also allow investors to invest in a larger set of securities with a smaller investment.
